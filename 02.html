ZahirPortraits

This sequence of portrait images was created for Zahir, an arts and politics magazine based in York. The portraits were intended to be distinctive and recognisable at a range of scales, and I worked using a clean, graphic style to ensure this.

I used the illustrations to explore the relationship between surface and form, carefully building up surfaces and planes through crosshatching in a way that suggests the mass and shape underneath. I used markers to block out large structures within each design, introducing texture and movement, and giving a contrast against the precise, tightly-controlled penwork used elsewhere.
